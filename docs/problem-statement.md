This will also showcase a practical application of IT, which includes UI/UX design, database management, and system design.

3.0 Activities

3.1 Problem Definition

The current healthcare appointment and consultation process remains inefficient, fragmented, and often inaccessible for many users. Despite the increasing adoption of digital health technologies, many healthcare providers still rely on outdated or poorly integrated systems that limit the effectiveness of telehealth services.
Patients frequently encounter challenges such as complicated booking procedures, long waiting times, and limited access to healthcare professionals, particularly in rural or remote areas. At the same time, healthcare providers struggle with managing appointments, maintaining accurate records, and ensuring secure communication across multiple disconnected systems.
The rapid digital transformation of healthcare, accelerated by the COVID-19 pandemic, has further exposed the lack of standardised and user-friendly telehealth platforms. Many existing solutions fail to provide a seamless experience that integrates scheduling, communication, and data management in a single system.
Therefore, the core problem addressed in this project is the lack of an efficient, secure, and user-friendly telehealth appointment platform that supports both patients and healthcare providers while ensuring accessibility and compliance with modern healthcare standards.

3.2 User Identification

The telehealth appointment platform is designed to support multiple user groups, each with specific needs and expectations.
Patients
Patients are the primary users of the platform. This includes individuals seeking convenient and timely access to healthcare services, particularly those with mobility limitations, busy schedules, or those living in rural and remote areas.
Their key needs include:
Simple and intuitive appointment booking
Access to healthcare services from home
Reliable virtual consultation features
Secure handling of personal health information 
Healthcare Providers
Healthcare providers include general practitioners (GPs), specialists, and allied health professionals who deliver medical services through the platform.
Their key needs include:
Efficient appointment and schedule management
Access to patient information and consultation history
Reliable communication tools for virtual consultations
Secure and compliant data handling systems
Healthcare Administrators
Administrators are responsible for managing operational aspects of healthcare facilities.
Their key needs include:
Monitoring appointment schedules
Managing patient records and system data
Generating reports and analytics
Ensuring compliance with healthcare regulations
Technical Support / IT Staff
This group is responsible for maintaining the platform’s performance, security, and reliability.
Their key needs include:
System monitoring and maintenance tools
Data security and backup management
Troubleshooting and technical support capabilities

This project is done with the vision to create a convenient healthcare appointment platform where patients can book and virtual consultations anytime and anywhere. The goals are to reduce waiting times, streamline appointment management and improve healthcare accessibility.

4.0 Questions To Answer

4.1 What Problem Are We Solving?

The problems with traditional booking methods, such as in-person, phone calls or emails, are they are slow, inconvenient and prone to scheduling errors. On top of that, some healthcare providers do not have a centralised system for managing telehealth appointments, leading to inefficiencies. 

With the Telehealth Appointment Platform, several problems will be solved, including difficulty finding available appointment times, long waiting times when attempting to book via phone or in-person, geographical barriers, missed appointments due to lack of reminders, double bookings, scheduling conflicts and long waiting times for walk-in patients.

The mentioned problems are addressed by providing online appointment booking which can be accessed anytime and anywhere, automated appointment confirmations and reminders, easy rescheduling or cancellation options and improved communication between patients and healthcare providers.

4.2 Who Experiences The Problem?

The primary targets of the aforementioned problem are patients all across Australia, seeking medical attention from trusted professionals and instead being met with inconsistent platforms that are not accessible and user friendly, making the booking process unnecessarily complex. On the other hand, doctors and staff employed at medical centres have also not been spared and encounter a similar set of problems when managing bookings and medical data. Since the nature of medical data is extremely sensitive, authentication is a necessary feature that must be implemented across all platforms to avoid any issues with compliance standards. As a result, all healthcare organizations and their customers are affected by these issues.

4.3 Why Does This Problem Exist?

Organizations have been unable to keep up with the rapid shift into digitalization of operations, thus leading to inconsistencies and a lack of standardization across healthcare systems which make them extremely difficult for consumers to use. Furthermore, many healthcare companies have been unable to transition out of legacy systems which are unable to integrate updated Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems into the current setup, making operation processing much more complex than it needs to be. The sensitive nature of healthcare data also makes it difficult to choose a suitable platform that will support the company’s operations while abiding by compliance standards.

4.4 Why Is It Important In Australia?

Australia has strict laws regarding healthcare and privacy (e.g Privacy Act 1988) that protect the rights of consumers and ensure the organizations abide by the enforced rules when it comes to handling data. Furthermore, the general Australian population is an extremely diverse demographic of people and diversity demands inclusivity. And finally, digitalization has also given way to a growing cybersecurity threat of which healthcare organizations are a primary target of, given the nature of the data they deal with. A standardized healthcare platform that abides by government standards and enforces authentication principles would be of great benefit to the general public. 

5.0 Solution

The proposed solution is a full-stack Telehealth Appointment Platform designed to directly address the inefficiencies, accessibility barriers, and security concerns identified in the problem definition. The platform will provide a centralised, secure, and user-friendly digital environment where patients can book appointments, attend virtual consultations, and manage their healthcare interaction, all from a single system. Healthcare providers and administrators will similarly benefit from streamlined scheduling, patient data management, and communication tools built into their respective dashboards.The platform will be built with scalability, compliance, and usability at its core, ensuring it meets the needs of a diverse Australian user base while adhering to relevant legislative standards such as the Privacy Act 1988 and Australian Digital Health Agency guidelines.

The platform follows a three-tier client-server architecture, separating the presentation layer(Frontend), application logic layer(Backend), and data layer(Database) to ensure modularity, maintainability, and scalability.The frontend will be built using Figma ,React.js and Tailwind CSS to create a clean and easy-to-use interface that works across different devices. The backend will use Node.js to manage the server-side logic and handle communication between the frontend and the database. PostgreSQL will be used to store data such as user profiles, appointments, and records.
The proposed telehealth platform  has four  core features:

Appointment Booking and Scheduling:
Patients can book appointments online anytime without phone calls or in-person visits. Real-time availability and conflict detection eliminate double bookings and scheduling errors.

Role-Based Dashboards
Each user gets a personalised dashboard showing only what is relevant to their role. This removes the need to juggle multiple disconnected systems.
 
Virtual Consultations 
Patients can attend appointments directly through the platform without any third-party tools. This is especially beneficial for patients in rural and remote areas of Australia.

Automated Reminders and Notifications 
The system automatically sends booking confirmations and reminders via email or in-platform alerts. This reduces missed appointments and helps providers manage their schedules more efficiently.

Additionally, Security is a foundational concern throughout the platform's design and implementation. All user inputs are validated and sanitised on both the frontend and backend to mitigate common vulnerabilities such as SQL injection and cross-site scripting (XSS) ,data sent between the user and the server will be encrypted(HTTPS/TLS protocol), passwords will be stored using bcrypt hashing, users will log in securely using JWT(JSON Web Tokens) authentication, which ensures each person only sees what is relevant to their role, .and the platform will comply with the Australian Privacy Act 1988 to protect patient information.
