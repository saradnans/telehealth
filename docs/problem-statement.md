# Problem Statement Document

## Problem Title
International Student Housing Finder

## Problem Description
International students coming to Australia often face significant difficulty finding safe, affordable, and convenient housing. Many arrive with limited knowledge of local suburbs, rental processes, transport systems, and pricing expectations. As a result, they can struggle to identify accommodation that is close to campus, within budget, and suitable for their needs.

The problem is not just about finding a room or apartment. It is also about making an informed decision in an unfamiliar environment. Students must often compare many listings across different platforms without clear guidance on location suitability, travel time, safety, or access to essential services.

This can lead to stress, poor housing choices, financial pressure, and a difficult transition into student life.

## Target Users
The main users affected by this problem are:
- international students planning to study in Australia
- newly arrived international students searching for accommodation quickly
- students with limited understanding of the Australian rental market

Secondary users may include:
- education agents supporting student relocation
- university support teams helping students settle in Australia

## Current Solutions
Current solutions include:
- general property rental websites
- Facebook groups and online community posts
- student accommodation websites
- word-of-mouth recommendations from friends or agents

These platforms provide housing information, but they are usually designed for a broad audience rather than specifically for international students.

## Why Current Solutions Fail
Current solutions often fail international students for several reasons:
- they do not focus on the specific needs of students
- they assume users already understand the Australian rental system
- they provide too many listings without enough context for decision-making
- they make it hard to compare housing options based on student priorities such as budget, travel time, and safety
- some sources are unreliable or difficult to verify
- important lifestyle factors, such as proximity to public transport and essential services, are not always easy to assess

Because of these gaps, international students may feel overwhelmed and make decisions based on incomplete or misleading information.

## Proposed Solution
The proposed solution is a platform called **International Student Housing Finder**.

This platform would help international students search for accommodation in Australia by offering a more student-focused experience. It would allow users to:
- search housing near universities or campuses
- filter options by price, room type, location, and lease terms
- compare shortlisted options in one place
- view useful context such as transport access, nearby supermarkets, and estimated travel times
- make housing decisions with more confidence

The project vision is to create a simple and practical tool that reduces uncertainty and improves the housing search experience for international students in Australia.

## Australian Market Context
This problem is important in Australia because the country attracts a large number of international students every year. Many students relocate from overseas and must secure housing in expensive and competitive rental markets. Cities such as Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane can be difficult to navigate without local knowledge.

In Australia, housing affordability, rental competition, and transport accessibility are major concerns. International students are especially vulnerable because they may face time pressure, limited inspection opportunities, and unfamiliar rental expectations. A solution designed specifically for this market can provide strong practical value.

## Project Vision
The vision of this project is to make the housing search process easier, clearer, and more reliable for international students in Australia. Instead of forcing students to piece together information from multiple sources, the platform would bring relevant housing insights together in one place.
